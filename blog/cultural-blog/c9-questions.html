Asking Good Questions


Communication Skills


5 Apr 15

What makes a good question? We all have a different answer to this, but when it comes to coding there are a couple of common attributes good questions share.

Title

The title must properly frame the question. The title should clearly and concisely provide the initial framing for the question in such a way that a potential answerer is not completely surprised by the content. If you are running into this (0.1 + 0.2 === .03 => false) error in JS, titling your post "Math not working" is a bad idea. "Errors in JS float arithmetic" is a much more descriptive and helpful title.

Problem

Clearly and concisely introduce your reader to the situation that created your problem (the context) and then state your problem. When providing context you should be sure to include and supporting code that is necessary to understanding the problem. Also, mention any environmental variables or programs you are running that have bearing on the problem. The problem itself should be what you are trying to achieve.

Writing

Be sure to double check your grammar and punctuation. Clarity and concision are achieved mostly through proper grammar.
