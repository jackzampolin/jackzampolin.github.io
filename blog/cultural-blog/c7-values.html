Affirmation and Stereotype Threat


Values


21 Mar 15

This week at DBC we were asked to think again about Stereotype threat. ReducingStereotypeThreat.org has a great list of strategies to help combat the negative affects of Stereotype Threat. The two I personally use are "encouraging self-affirmation", and "emphasizing high standards with assurances about capability for meeting them." The values I list below, all of which I try to live up to, help build a foundation to resist the negative influences of stereotype threat.

Family/Friendships/Close Relationships

As the quote goes, "You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with." Having close, nurturing relationships is a crucial part of living a healthy and fulfilling life.

Honesty/Integrety

Living your life with honesty and integrity is, I believe, extremely important. Being honest with others is an extremely important, but honesty and integrity in your dealings with yourself is just as important. If you lie to yourself it is easy to do things that don't align with your values. Self-deception also stunts your growth personally and creatively.

Introspection/Personal Development/Growth

Continuing to grow personally and otherwise is extremely important to me. Introspection and personal development are both tools that I use to try and facilitate that growth. These are all hard without self honesty.

Courage/Adventure

I think the world is filled with wonder and exploring it is one of my favorite things. Courage is required in any good adventure because you run into obstacles and tests that must be overcome if you want to reach your goals. "Courage is the latter on which all the other virtues mount."

Excellence/Craftsmanship

Making nice things requires a dedication to the craft of doing so. It also requires that you hold yourself to a standard of excellence that is above what is initially achievable. That is how you get better.
