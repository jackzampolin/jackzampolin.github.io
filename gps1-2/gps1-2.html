Pair Programming at DBC

By Jack Zampolin and Nathan Werbeckes


What is Pair Programming?

When two programmers work together to create or improve code. In this collaborative process there are two roles a driver and a navigator. These roles are fluid and can switch multiple times during a pair session.

Comparison of Driver and Navigator Roles

Driver

The driver is the one on the keyboard doing the physical typing of the code. Their role is to be in the weeds and ensure the correct syntax is being used and that the program will succesfully compile/run.

Navigator

The navigator is responsible for ensuring that the driver is on the right course and assisting the driver in any way they can. They primarily help with looking up methods or routines to accomplish the task at hand or review the brief.

Importance of Pair Programming at DBC

One of the primary benefits is that having two sets of eyes on the code will reduce mistakes. Pair programming is becoming more popular across the industry and DBC wants to expose us to this process. Having two people prevents signifcant writer's block and helps keep up the energy and focus of both team members.
